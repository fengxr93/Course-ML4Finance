# Machine Learning for Finance

This course explores the intersection of machine learning (ML) and finance, with applications spanning asset pricing, portfolio optimization, risk management, market microstructure, and more. Students will learn how to apply state-of-the-art ML techniques to financial data and gain practical experience through hands-on projects.

---

## Quick Info
- **Course format:** Lectures • Case studies • Hands-on projects
- **Prerequisites:**  
  - Background in finance or economics  
  - Familiarity with machine learning, applied statistics, and Python
---

## Learning Objectives
By the end of the course, you will be able to:
1. Formulate finance problems as data-driven predictive or decision tasks.
2. Build, tune, and evaluate ML models on financial datasets (cross-sectional and time-series).
3. Understand the strengths/limitations of linear and nonlinear models in noisy, non-stationary markets.
4. Implement high-frequency modeling pipelines using limit order book (LOB) data.
5. Design and backtest portfolio strategies with realistic frictions and risk controls.
6. Apply reinforcement learning and generative models to finance use cases.
7. Communicate empirical findings and model risks clearly and reproducibly.

## Course Agenda (Dates Removed)
- **Lecture 1: Introduction to Financial Machine Learning**  
  Overview; prices as predictions; information sets; functional forms; practical challenges.

- **Lecture 2: Empirical Asset Pricing I**  
  Financial data; experimental design; simple/penalized linear models; dimension reduction. *(HW1 released)*

- **Lecture 3: Empirical Asset Pricing II**  
  Decision trees & ensembles (bias/variance/noise); selected papers; neural networks; alternative data (image/text).

- **Lecture 4: High-Frequency Return Prediction**  
  Financial time series; LOB, sampling frequency; order flow; universality & cross impact; spatio-temporal modeling. *(HW2 released)*

- **Lecture 5: Optimal Portfolios**  
  Plug-in methods; integrated estimation & optimization; max-Sharpe regression; trading costs.

- **Lecture 6: Reinforcement Learning in Finance**  
  RL fundamentals; execution & market making; selected ML papers; project proposal check-in.

- **Lecture 7: Generative AI for Synthetic Financial Data**  
  GANs/diffusion/VAEs; backtesting with synthetic data; pitfalls; overview of TailGAN/MARS.

- **Lecture 8: Risk Management with Graphs**  
  Graph ML; VaR; credit risk (fraud/anomaly/defaults/credit scoring); systemic risk.

- **Lecture 9: Large Language Models (LLMs)**  
  Sentiment; report generation; chatbots; reasoning; challenges.

- **Lecture 10: Interpretability & Ethics**  
  Visualization (clustering, t-SNE, graph DBs); LIME/SHAP; bias/fairness; transparency; compliance.

- **Lecture 11: Advanced Topics**  
  Transfer learning; federated learning; market microstructure & stat-arb; backtesting pipeline.

- **Lecture 12: Case Studies & Hands-On**  
  HF prediction, volatility forecasting, LLM applications (guided lab).

- **Lecture 13: Final Project Presentations**

## Assignments
- **HW1 (Cross-Sectional Returns):**  
  Assess linear regression (strengths/limits) and predict stock returns using U.S. equity data.
- **HW2 (High-Frequency Modeling):**  
  Work with LOB data; replicate and analyze the DeepLOB model.

## Final Project
Choose one of the following, or propose your own topic (LLMs in finance encouraged):

- **Option 1: Generate OHLC Charts**  
  *Data:* Provided historical price data.  
  *Outcome:* Build a generative model for OHLC sequences.

- **Option 2: Statistical Arbitrage with LOB Data**  
  *Data:* Raw LOBSTER data (provided).  
  *Outcome:* Develop and evaluate a data-driven stat-arb strategy.

- **Propose Your Own (LLM-centric ideas welcome):**  
  - Automated financial news summarization  
  - Financial report generation  
  - Sentiment analysis for stock prediction  
  - Other LLM applications in finance

**Deliverables:** short proposal, code repo, reproducible notebooks, report, and in-class presentation.
